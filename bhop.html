Bunny Hopping (BHOP)

Master the art of movement and gain speed through consecutive jumps

What is Bunny Hopping?

Bunny hopping, commonly known as "bhop," is an advanced movement technique in Counter-Strike 2 that allows players to maintain and increase velocity by chaining consecutive jumps while strafing. This technique exploits the game's physics engine to move faster than normal running speed.

When executed correctly, bunny hopping enables players to traverse maps more quickly, making them harder targets to hit while maintaining offensive capabilities. It's a fundamental skill that separates casual players from competitive ones.

Proper bunny hopping technique in action

How It Works

The physics behind bunny hopping relies on air strafing and precise timing. When a player jumps, they can gain speed by moving the mouse in sync with their strafe direction. The key is to jump immediately upon landing to maintain momentum without friction slowing you down.

Core Mechanics

  • Air Strafing: Moving your mouse left while pressing A, or right while pressing D
  • Jump Timing: Pressing jump the exact moment you touch the ground
  • Momentum Conservation: Each successful jump maintains or increases speed
  • Maximum Velocity: CS2 has speed caps to prevent excessive acceleration

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start Running: Begin by running forward (W key)
  2. First Jump: Press spacebar and immediately release W
  3. Air Strafe: While in the air, hold A and smoothly move mouse left (or D with mouse right)
  4. Land and Jump: The moment you land, jump again
  5. Alternate Strafes: Switch between A and D strafes for maximum speed
  6. Maintain Rhythm: Keep a consistent timing between jumps

Pro Tips

Bind Jump to Scroll Wheel: Most professionals bind jump to mouse wheel up or down for easier timing. This allows you to hit the perfect jump timing more consistently.

Practice on KZ Maps: Kreedz climbing maps are specifically designed to help you master movement mechanics including bhop.

Don't Over-Rotate: Moving your mouse too much during air strafes will actually slow you down. Keep movements smooth and controlled.

Common Mistakes

❌ Holding W in the Air

This is the most common mistake. You should never hold W after the initial jump, as it prevents proper air strafing.

❌ Inconsistent Jump Timing

Jumping too early or too late breaks momentum. Practice the rhythm until it becomes muscle memory.

Practical Applications

In competitive matches, bunny hopping serves several strategic purposes:

  • Faster Rotations: Move between bomb sites or defensive positions more quickly
  • Unpredictable Movement: Harder for enemies to track and hit you
  • Peeking Advantage: Gain speed before peeking corners for wider peeks
  • Economy of Time: Reach positions before opponents, especially important in post-plant situations

⚠️ CS2 vs CS:GO Differences

In CS2, bunny hopping has been slightly adjusted compared to CS:GO. The velocity cap is enforced more strictly, and landing mechanics have been refined with the new Source 2 engine. Players report that while bhop is still viable, it requires more precise timing than in previous versions.
