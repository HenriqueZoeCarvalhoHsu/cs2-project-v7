Boosting

Teamwork to reach elevated positions and gain tactical advantages

Understanding Boosting

Boosting is a fundamental team mechanic in Counter-Strike 2 where one player stands on top of another teammate to reach elevated positions that would otherwise be inaccessible. This technique creates unexpected angles, provides information advantages, and can completely change round dynamics.

Professional teams have specific boost spots memorized for every map and practice coordinated boosts as part of their tactical playbook. A well-timed boost can catch enemies completely off-guard, turning impossible positions into devastating advantages.

Coordinated boost providing elevated sightline advantage

How Boosting Works

The mechanics are simple but require coordination:

Basic Boost Setup

  1. Bottom Player: Positions at boost spot and crouches (Hold Ctrl)
  2. Top Player: Jumps on teammate's head while crouching
  3. Stabilization: Both players remain still and crouched for stability
  4. Execution: Top player has elevated view and shooting position

Types of Boosts

Standard Boost (2 Players)

The most common boost involving two players. One crouches while the other stands on their head, gaining approximately 1-2 meters of height. Used for peeking over walls, boxes, or getting better angles on enemies.

Common Uses: Checking positions, surprise angles, information gathering

Triple Boost (3 Players)

Stacking three players vertically for extreme height advantages. Bottom player crouches, second player stands on them, third player on top. Rarely used but devastating when unexpected. Requires excellent communication and positioning.

Common Uses: Skybox visibility, extreme off-angles, specific map exploits

Self-Boost (Solo)

Using objects, geometry, or specific jumping techniques to reach elevated positions alone. Includes pixel boosts, edge boosts, and jump-crouch combinations that don't require teammates.

Common Uses: Solo plays, saving situations, quick repositioning

Strategic Boost Spots by Map

Every competitive map has key boost positions that teams must know:

Offensive Boosts

Used by attackers to gain sight advantages:

  • Mirage: Connector boost for mid control
  • Dust II: Short boost for A site vision
  • Inferno: Coffin boost for A site entry
  • Overpass: Bathrooms boost for B control

Defensive Boosts

Used by defenders for holding positions:

  • Cache: Checkers boost for A site defense
  • Nuke: Silo boost for outside control
  • Vertigo: Ramp boost for mid control
  • Ancient: Various temple boosts

Advanced Boosting Techniques

Quick Boost

Rapidly executing boosts under time pressure. Both players must communicate and position instantly. Often used during fast executes or when reacting to enemy movements. Requires significant practice to execute cleanly.

Key Skills: Fast communication, precise positioning, immediate execution

Combat Boost

Maintaining boost position while under fire. The bottom player must stay stable despite taking damage or being flashed. Top player must frag while maintaining balance. High difficulty but extremely effective when successful.

Challenges: Taking damage breaks position, aim instability, vulnerability

Fake Boost

Setting up boost position to bait enemy utility or attention, then not actually boosting. Forces enemies to check boost positions or waste grenades. Psychological warfare that makes future boosts more effective.

Purpose: Utility bait, information denial, mind games

Communication is Critical

Boost Callouts

"Boost me [location]": Requesting boost at specific spot

"Coming for boost": Moving to boost position

"I'm bottom/top": Clarifying roles

"Hold position": Bottom player must stay stable

"Drop down": Dismounting after information gathered

Clear communication prevents failed boosts and maintains team coordination.

Risks and Considerations

Boost Vulnerabilities

  • Immobility: Both players are stationary targets
  • Resource Investment: Takes two players out of rotation
  • Utility Vulnerable: Single grenade can hit both players
  • Dismount Time: Takes time to get back into normal positions
  • Predictability: Once known, enemies pre-fire boost spots

Timing Your Boosts

Boost timing is crucial for success:

  • Early Round: Information boosts to gather enemy positions
  • Mid Round: Surprise angles when enemies are committed elsewhere
  • Late Round: Desperation boosts or post-plant positions
  • After Utility: Boost immediately after smokes deploy
  • Eco Rounds: Cheap boost plays when economy is poor

Practice Tips

Custom Games: Practice boost spots with teammates in private servers.

Quick Setup: Time how fast you can execute each boost cleanly.

Map Knowledge: Explore every map to find new boost opportunities.

Pro Demo Study: Watch how professionals use boosts in matches.

Height Testing: Test what angles each boost reveals before using in matches.

Famous Boost Spots

Legendary Boosts in CS History

Olofmeister's Overpass Boost: The most controversial boost in CS history during Fnatic vs. LDLC at DreamHack 2014. Players boosted to skybox level on A site, seeing the entire map. This boost was later patched and the match replayed.

Train Ivy Boost: A classic boost that's been part of Train since 1.6, allowing views over the entire B site.

Mirage Tetris Boost: Provides vision over ramp and stairs, commonly used in professional play.

Countering Boosts

When you suspect enemy boosts:

  • Pre-fire Common Spots: Check known boost positions
  • Utility Clearing: HE grenades and molotovs force boosts down
  • Flank Positions: Boost spots often vulnerable from sides
  • Information Sharing: Call out boosts immediately for team adjustment
  • Avoid Predictability: Don't walk into boosted sightlines
