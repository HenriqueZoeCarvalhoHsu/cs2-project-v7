Spray Control & Recoil

Master weapon patterns to eliminate multiple enemies

Understanding Recoil Mechanics

Spray control, also known as recoil control, is the technique of compensating for weapon recoil patterns to maintain accuracy during sustained fire. In CS2, every weapon has a fixed recoil pattern that remains consistent, meaning you can learn and master it through practice.

Unlike many shooters with random spray patterns, Counter-Strike rewards players who invest time learning these patterns. Professional players can control 20+ bullet sprays with pinpoint accuracy, often securing multi-kills in critical rounds.

Visual representation of spray patterns - each weapon has unique behavior

How Recoil Works in CS2

When you hold down the trigger, bullets don't go where your crosshair is pointing after the first shot. The weapon kicks up and to the sides following a predetermined pattern. To maintain accuracy, you must move your mouse in the opposite direction of this pattern.

Key Recoil Concepts

  • First Shot Accuracy: The first bullet is always accurate (with proper crosshair placement)
  • Pattern Consistency: Each weapon has a fixed pattern that repeats
  • Compensation: Move mouse opposite to the pattern (pull down for upward recoil)
  • Spray Duration: Longer sprays become exponentially harder to control

Main Weapon Spray Patterns

AK-47 Pattern

Difficulty: Hard

Pattern: Vertical climb ‚Üí Right ‚Üí Left zigzag

The AK-47 is the iconic terrorist rifle. Its pattern shoots straight up for the first 8-10 bullets, then begins moving right and left in a zigzag. Control requires:

  • Pull straight down for first 10 bullets
  • Slight left compensation around bullet 11
  • Alternating left-right micro-adjustments

M4A4/M4A1-S Pattern

Difficulty: Medium

Pattern: Vertical ‚Üí Slight right

CT rifles have easier patterns than the AK. The M4A4 and M4A1-S climb mostly vertical with minor horizontal deviation:

  • Pull down consistently throughout spray
  • Very slight right compensation mid-spray
  • M4A1-S is more forgiving than M4A4

MP9/MAC-10 Pattern

Difficulty: Easy

Pattern: Tight vertical climb

SMG spray patterns are simpler and more forgiving:

  • Mostly vertical recoil
  • Easy to control at close range
  • High fire rate makes timing important

AWP (No Pattern)

Difficulty: N/A

Type: Bolt-action

The AWP has no spray pattern as it's single-shot:

  • 100% first shot accuracy when scoped
  • Must scope between shots for accuracy
  • Movement heavily affects accuracy

Spray vs. Tap vs. Burst

Different situations call for different firing techniques:

Spray (Full Auto)

Use When:

  • Close to medium range
  • Multiple enemies grouped
  • Committed to fight
  • Need suppressive fire

Control: Requires pattern knowledge

Bursting (3-5 Bullets)

Use When:

  • Medium to long range
  • Enemy partially visible
  • Unsure of commitment
  • Need quick reset

Control: Pull down, pause, repeat

Tap Firing (Single Shots)

Use When:

  • Long range engagements
  • Precise shots needed
  • Enemy at extreme distance
  • First shot critical

Control: Wait for recoil reset between shots (0.35s for rifles)

Advanced Spray Techniques

Spray Transfer

The ability to continue a spray from one enemy to another without resetting. When you kill the first target mid-spray, immediately track to the next enemy while continuing your recoil compensation. This is incredibly difficult but devastating when executed correctly.

Practice: Use aim training maps with multiple bots to practice transferring between targets.

Commit or Reset

Deciding whether to commit to a spray or reset is crucial. If your first bullets miss, continuing a bad spray usually results in death. Learn to recognize when to stop, counter-strafe, and reset your accuracy.

Rule of Thumb: If first 5 bullets miss, reset and reposition.

Moving Spray

While spraying, use counter-strafing between bursts to adjust position. Spray 3-5 bullets, counter-strafe to stop, adjust position, spray again. This makes you harder to hit while maintaining offensive pressure.

Training Spray Control

Practice Routine

1. Wall Practice (5 minutes): Spray into a wall and try to keep bullets in a tight group. Start slow, focusing on the pattern.

2. Recoil Master Map (10 minutes): Use the workshop map "Recoil Master" which shows the pattern and tracks your accuracy.

3. Bot Practice (10 minutes): Use "Aim Botz" to practice spraying moving targets at various ranges.

4. Deathmatch (20 minutes): Apply spray control in real combat scenarios. Focus on technique over score.

Common Spray Mistakes

Avoid These Errors

  • Over-Compensating: Pulling down too hard causes bullets to go low
  • Spraying Too Long: After 15 bullets, accuracy drops dramatically
  • Ignoring Distance: Long-range sprays are almost always wrong
  • Static Spraying: Standing still while spraying makes you an easy target
  • Wrong Weapon Choice: Using spray weapons at sniper distances
  • Not Crouching: Crouching reduces recoil significantly

Crouching and Spray

Crouching in CS2 provides several spray benefits:

  • Reduced Recoil: Patterns become tighter and more controllable
  • Lower Profile: Dodge headshots if enemy aims high
  • Commitment Signal: Crouching commits you to the fight

Weapon-Specific Tips

AK-47 Mastery

  • First 10 bullets: straight down
  • Bullet 11-15: down and slightly left
  • Beyond 15: micro left-right adjustments
  • Practice 10-bullet sprays first

M4 Mastery

  • More forgiving vertical pattern
  • Consistent downward pull
  • Less horizontal adjustment needed
  • Can spray longer than AK reliably

CS2 Spray Changes

CS2's Source 2 engine brought subtle changes to spray mechanics:

  • Subtick System: More accurate registration of spray patterns
  • Visual Feedback: Improved tracers and bullet impacts
  • Consistent Patterns: Patterns feel more reliable than CS:GO
  • Movement Inaccuracy: Slightly adjusted penalties for moving while shooting

üí° Professional Secret

Top-tier professionals don't spray beyond 10-12 bullets in most situations. They either kill the enemy in that time or reset their spray. Learning when NOT to spray is as important as learning the patterns themselves. Quality over quantity.

Spray Transfer Mastery

The ultimate spray skill is transferring between multiple enemies. When your first target dies mid-spray, you're already 5-10 bullets into the pattern. To transfer:

  1. Maintain recoil compensation
  2. Flick crosshair to next target
  3. Continue pattern from where you stopped
  4. Adjust for distance to new target

Final Practice Tip

Watch professional POV demos in slow motion (use demo_timescale 0.5 in console). Observe how pros control sprays, when they commit, and when they reset. Their decision-making in spray situations is as important as their mechanical control.
