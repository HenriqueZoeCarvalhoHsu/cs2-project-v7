Grenade Mechanics

Master utility to control the map and win rounds

Understanding Utility

Grenades, commonly called "utility," are essential tools in Counter-Strike 2 that often determine round outcomes more than raw aim. Professional teams spend countless hours practicing grenade lineups and coordinated utility usage. Mastering grenades separates tactical players from those who rely solely on gunplay.

In CS2, grenades have been refined with the Source 2 engine, featuring improved physics, realistic smoke behaviors, and enhanced visual effects. Understanding each grenade type and how to use them effectively is crucial for competitive play.

The complete arsenal of grenades available in CS2

Grenade Types Overview

CS2 features five types of grenades, each serving specific tactical purposes. Players can carry one of each type (except flashbangs, which allow two):

HE Grenade

Cost: $300

Damage: Up to 98 HP (center of explosion)

High Explosive grenades deal damage in a radius. Perfect for:

  • Damaging grouped enemies
  • Forcing enemies to move
  • Finishing low-health opponents
  • Clearing common hiding spots

Smoke Grenade

Cost: $300

Duration: ~18 seconds

Creates a smoke cloud that blocks vision. Essential for:

  • Blocking sniper sightlines
  • Executing bomb site takes
  • Safe bomb plants
  • Cutting off map sections
  • Denying information to enemies

Flashbang

Cost: $200

Max Carry: 2

Blinds players looking at it. Critical for:

  • Entry fragging
  • Peeking aggressive angles
  • Retaking bomb sites
  • Stopping rushes
  • Creating openings in stalemates

Molotov / Incendiary

Cost: $400 (T) / $600 (CT)

Duration: 7 seconds

Creates a fire that damages and denies area. Used for:

  • Delaying pushes
  • Clearing corners
  • Post-plant situations
  • Forcing position changes
  • Time wasting

Decoy Grenade

Cost: $50

Duration: 15 seconds

Mimics gunfire and shows on radar. Useful for:

  • Faking presence elsewhere
  • Baiting enemy rotations
  • Creating audio distractions
  • Budget utility rounds

Advanced Grenade Techniques

Throwing Mechanics

CS2 offers three throw types, each with different trajectories and applications:

Three Throw Types

  • Left Click (Full Throw): Maximum distance and speed. Use for long-range lineups.
  • Right Click (Underhand): Short distance with arc. Perfect for close-range precise placements.
  • Both Clicks (Medium): Mid-range throw combining speed and arc. Most versatile option.

Jump Throws

Jump throws allow grenades to reach positions impossible with standing throws. In CS2, jump throw binds are built into the game, making them tournament-legal and consistent.

Setting Up Jump Throw

Command: Add to your autoexec.cfg:

alias "+jumpthrow" "+jump;-attack"

alias "-jumpthrow" "-jump"

bind "x" "+jumpthrow"

Replace "x" with your preferred key. This ensures consistent jump throws every time.

Smoke Lineups

Smoke grenades require precise lineups to consistently land in strategic locations. Professional teams have dozens of smoke lineups memorized for each map.

Deep Smokes

These land deep in defended areas, blocking CT rotations and off-angles. Essential for site executes. Examples include Mirage CT spawn smoke or Inferno library smoke.

One-Way Smokes

Partially deployed smokes on elevated surfaces allow defenders to see attackers' feet while remaining hidden. These are powerful but require perfect placement and understanding of sight lines.

Fast Smokes

Quick, on-the-run smokes that don't require perfect lineups. Used for improvisation and fast executes where precision is sacrificed for speed.

Flashbang Techniques

Pop Flashes

Flashbangs that explode immediately upon entering the enemy's view, giving no time to turn away. Bounce them off walls, ceilings, or throw them high to minimize exposure time.

Team Flashes

Coordinated flashes where a teammate throws while you peek. Communication is crucial. Call "flashing for you" so teammates know to peek aggressively while enemies are blinded.

Always communicate before throwing flashbangs. In CS2, team flash damage has been adjusted to discourage careless utility usage. Call out your flashes clearly and wait for teammates to acknowledge before throwing.

Molotov Strategies

Post-Plant Molotovs

After planting the bomb, molotovs delay defuse attempts. Learn to throw molotovs that cover the bomb from safe positions. This forces CTs to wait or take damage, burning precious seconds off the clock.

Clearing Molotovs

Throw molotovs into corners and common hiding spots before entering areas. Enemies must either reveal themselves or take significant damage. Particularly effective in close quarters like Inferno apartments.

Extinguishing with Smokes

Smoke grenades extinguish molotov fires instantly in CS2. This creates interesting counterplay where attackers molly, defenders smoke it out, then attackers re-molly. This utility exchange is crucial in high-level play.

Utility Economy

Managing utility purchases is as important as weapon economy:

  • Full Buy Rounds: Purchase complete utility set ($1000+ in grenades)
  • Force Buy: Priority to smokes and flashes, skip HE and molotov if needed
  • Eco Rounds: Save utility for next round, unless a specific play requires it
  • Anti-Eco: Molotovs and HE grenades are particularly effective against unarmored opponents

Practice Methods

Practice Mode: Use sv_cheats 1 and sv_infinite_ammo 1 to practice lineups without time pressure.

Lineup Maps: Download workshop maps dedicated to grenade lineups for each map.

Watch Pros: Study professional demos to learn which utility they prioritize in different situations.

Custom Games: Practice utility executes with your team in private servers.

Advanced Utility Coordination

Solo utility is good; coordinated team utility is game-changing. Professional teams synchronize utility to completely overwhelm defenders:

Standard Site Execute

  1. Smoke Deep Positions: Block CT rotations and off-angles
  2. Molotov Common Spots: Force defenders out of default positions
  3. Flash Entry Points: Blind defenders as team enters
  4. HE Grenade Spam: Damage grouped defenders
  5. Execute Together: Enter site immediately after utility lands

CS2 Smoke Mechanics Update

CS2 introduced revolutionary smoke mechanics that react to the environment:

  • Volumetric Smokes: Smokes are now 3D volumes rather than sprites
  • Bullet Interaction: Bullets temporarily create holes in smoke clouds
  • HE Grenade Clearing: HE grenades temporarily disperse smoke
  • Dynamic Movement: Smokes react to wind and explosions realistically
