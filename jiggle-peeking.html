Jiggle Peeking

Gather information while being nearly impossible to hit

What is Jiggle Peeking?

Jiggle peeking is an advanced movement technique where players rapidly peek and unpeak from cover using quick A-D strafing movements. This technique allows you to gather information about enemy positions while presenting an extremely difficult target to hit. It's particularly effective against AWPers and players holding static angles.

When executed properly, jiggle peeking makes you appear for only a fraction of a secondâ€”barely enough time for enemies to react, let alone shoot accurately. Professional players use this technique constantly to safely check angles without committing to full peeks.

Rapid peeking motion makes the player nearly impossible to hit

The Mechanics Behind It

Jiggle peeking relies on fast strafing combined with peekers advantage and network latency. On your screen, you see around corners before defenders see you on theirs. By immediately returning to cover, you deny them the opportunity to shoot back effectively.

Key Components

  • Quick Strafing: Rapid A-D-A-D movement around corners
  • Minimal Exposure: Only expose yourself for 0.2-0.3 seconds
  • Peekers Advantage: You see enemies before they see you
  • Information Gathering: Spot enemies without committing to fights

How to Perform Jiggle Peeks

  1. Position Near Cover: Stand close to the wall or corner you'll peek from
  2. Strafe Out (D): Quickly tap D to peek right side of cover
  3. Immediate Return (A): Instantly tap A to return to safety
  4. Repeat: Continue A-D pattern to gather more information
  5. Counter-Strafe: Use proper counter-strafing if you want to shoot during peek

Timing is Critical

The effectiveness of jiggle peeking depends on speed. Each peek should be extremely briefâ€”just long enough to see what's there. Think of it as "flickering" in and out of view rather than peeking.

Types of Jiggle Peeks

Information Jiggle

Pure information gathering with no intention to shoot. Use this to safely check if anyone is holding an angle. Extremely fast peeks that don't even stop to aim. Perfect for checking AWP positions or confirming enemy locations before committing teammates.

Combat Jiggle

Slightly slower peeks where you counter-strafe and potentially fire. Each peek is an opportunity to take a shot. Alternate between peeking and shooting, making yourself difficult to hit while still applying pressure. Requires excellent counter-strafing skills.

Shoulder Peek

An extreme version where only your shoulder model is visible. Used specifically to bait AWP shots without exposing yourself to danger. After the AWPer misses, you can aggressively peek while they're scoped out or reloading.

Advanced Applications

Against AWPs

Jiggle peeking is devastating against AWPs because:

  • AWPs need time to react and shoot
  • Missing punishes them with reload time
  • You can bait shots and immediately punish
  • Forces AWPer to reposition or give up angle

Against Rifles

Still effective but requires different approach:

  • Faster peeks required
  • More emphasis on shoulder peeks
  • Can't bait shots as reliably
  • Use to gather info before wide peek

Common Mistakes

  • Peeking Too Wide: Exposing too much of your body defeats the purpose
  • Too Slow: If enemy can track and shoot you, it's not a jiggle peek
  • Predictable Rhythm: Varying your timing makes it harder to predict
  • Not Using Information: Jiggle peeking is worthless if you don't act on what you learn
  • Overusing: Good players will predict and pre-fire jiggle peeks

Practice Drills

Bot Practice: Set up bots holding angles and practice jiggle peeking without getting hit.

Timing Test: Record yourself jiggle peeking and count frames of exposure. Aim for under 10 frames at 60 FPS.

Peek Variations: Practice wide peeks, jiggle peeks, and shoulder peeks from the same position to build variety.

Live Practice: Use in deathmatch against real players to test effectiveness under pressure.

Countering Jiggle Peeks

Understanding how to counter jiggle peeks makes you a better player:

  • Don't Overcommit: Don't waste shots on jiggle peeks
  • Pre-Fire Timing: Anticipate the peek and pre-fire
  • Reposition: After being jiggle peeked, change your angle
  • Utility Usage: Molotov or grenade the peeking position
  • Aggressive Counter: Push through their jiggle peek while they're out of position

CS2 Specific Considerations

The subtick system in CS2 affects jiggle peeking:

  • More Precise Registration: Peeks must be faster than in CS:GO
  • Peekers Advantage Adjusted: Slightly different timing windows
  • Movement Feel: Source 2 movement feels different, requiring adaptation
  • Higher Skill Ceiling: Proper execution is more rewarding
